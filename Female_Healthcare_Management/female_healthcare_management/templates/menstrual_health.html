Oligomenorrhea is a menstrual disorder characterized by infrequent or irregular menstrual periods. It may result from various factors, including hormonal imbalances, stress, or underlying health conditions. Symptoms may include missed periods, lighter menstrual flow, and symptoms related to hormonal imbalance. Treatment options can include lifestyle changes, hormonal therapy, or medication, depending on the underlying cause.

Fibroids are noncancerous growths of the uterus that can cause heavy menstrual bleeding, prolonged periods, and pelvic pain. While the exact cause is unknown, they are influenced by hormonal changes, particularly estrogen. Treatment options may include medication, lifestyle changes, and surgical procedures if fibroids cause significant symptoms.

Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS) involves physical and emotional symptoms that occur before menstruation. Symptoms may include mood swings, bloating, headaches, and fatigue. While the exact cause of PMS is unclear, it is believed to be linked to hormonal fluctuations. Management strategies can include lifestyle changes, dietary modifications, and medications to alleviate symptoms.

Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD) is a severe form of PMS that significantly impacts daily functioning. Symptoms include intense mood swings, irritability, depression, and anxiety. PMDD is caused by hormonal changes and can be diagnosed based on specific criteria. Treatment options include lifestyle changes, antidepressants, and hormonal therapies.

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder common among women of reproductive age. Symptoms can include irregular periods, excess hair growth, acne, and weight gain. The exact cause is unknown but may involve genetic and environmental factors. Management may include lifestyle changes, medications to regulate menstrual cycles, and treatments for associated symptoms.

Endometriosis is a painful condition in which tissue similar to the lining of the uterus grows outside the uterus. Symptoms can include chronic pelvic pain, painful periods, and fertility issues. The exact cause is unclear, but it may involve genetic and hormonal factors. Treatment options can range from pain relief medications to hormonal therapies and surgical interventions.
